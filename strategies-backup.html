Gap and Close Strategy

A mean-reversion strategy that exploits the statistical tendency of gap openings to partially or fully close during the trading session. This approach is particularly effective during periods of market overreaction and emotional trading.

Strategy Fundamentals

  • Gap Classification: Systematic categorization of gaps by magnitude, catalyst, and market context
  • Probability Analysis: Historical pattern recognition to determine gap-fill probability
  • Entry Optimization: Precise timing based on intraday reversal signals and momentum divergence
  • Target Management: Dynamic profit targets based on gap size and market volatility

Market Edge Identification

  • Earnings gaps with sentiment overreaction (74% fill rate within 3 days)
  • News-driven gaps without fundamental justification
  • Market open gaps exceeding 2.5 standard deviations from mean
  • Sector-specific gaps during broader market stability
  • Post-FOMC gaps with mean-reverting characteristics
  • Friday close to Monday open gaps (weekend effect exploitation)

Execution Excellence

  • Statistical Validation: Real-time probability calculations based on 10+ years of gap data
  • Market Microstructure: Order book analysis to optimize entry and exit timing
  • Volatility Adaptation: Dynamic strategy parameters based on realized and implied volatility
  • Regime Detection: Market regime classification to adjust strategy aggressiveness

Performance Metrics

  • Sharpe ratio: 2.8 over 5-year backtest period
  • Maximum consecutive losses: 7 (robust drawdown control)
  • Average trade duration: 4.2 hours
  • Market neutrality: Low correlation to broader market movements
